UX Knowledge Base

Learn how apps manipulate your behavior

Infinite Scroll

Eliminates natural "stopping cues." Your brain never gets the signal to say "I'm done reading," trapping you in a bottomless feed designed to bypass executive function.

NeuroGuard Intervention: 15-min hard locks re-introduce digital friction.

Variable Reward (Slot Machine UX)

Pull-to-refresh mimics slot machines. You pull without knowing if the reward will be a viral video or a dull post, creating a dopamine-driven "near-miss" addiction loop.

NeuroGuard Intervention: Replaces refreshing with mindful Bubble-Pop breaks.

Artificial FOMO & Ghost Notifications

Red notifications evoke urgency. Platforms use "ghost" alerts (fake engagement) or disappearing stories to create a biological pressure to check the app constantly.

NeuroGuard Intervention: Redirects biological urgency into focused Pomodoro work.

Nagging & Forced Opt-In

Disruptive prompts that repeatedly ask for location or contacts until you comply. They capitalize on decision fatigue to wear down your privacy defenses.

NeuroGuard Intervention: Tracks system-level permission prompts to flag invasive app behavior.

Emotional Steering (Guilt-Tripping)

Using manipulative language like "Your friends will miss you" when you try to deactivate a setting. It's designed to trigger social shame and prevent exit.

NeuroGuard Intervention: Parent Alerts provide objective oversight against emotional hooks.

Roach Motel (Hard-to-Cancel)

Seamless sign-ups but intentionally complex cancellation flows. They hide the "Exit" button behind multiple menu layers to exploit inertia.

NeuroGuard Intervention: Hard-Lock bypasses app navigation to force an immediate session exit.
